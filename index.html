As a recent graduate of the Women and Gender Studies program at SFSU, I have been equipped  with the tools and practices to engage with the world, globally and locally. Throughout my educational career I have developed the ability to lead group projects, forums, as well as build rapport with those around me, and to grasp abstract concepts while realizing them as real world results.

Outside of acadamia, I mentored a group of young Muslim women from diverse backgrounds to open up and work together in creating their first ever publication, a zine entitled: She Rose Above. After my mentees presented their work, they sold their zines and donated the proceeds to a women’s shelter.

Whether I am mentoring young women, empowering under-resourced students to express themselves through art, or updating a non-profit’s website, my work carries meaning behind it. I love building and maintaining connections with people, being an active community maker, listening to peoples stories, and learning new coffee brewing techniques.
